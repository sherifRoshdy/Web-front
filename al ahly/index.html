It is known as "The Club of the Century" in African football.[1] It is best known for its professional football team that plays in the Egyptian Premier League, the top tier in the Egyptian football league system, and is the most decorated football club in Africa.[2][3] It was founded on 24 April 1907, as a gathering place for Cairo's Student Unions. Al Ahly has a record of 41 national league titles, 36 national cups titles, and 11 national super cup titles making Al Ahly the most decorated club in Egypt. In addition, Al Ahly has never been relegated to the Egyptian Second Division. In international competitions, the club has won a record 8 CAF Champions League titles, a CAF Confederation Cup title, a record 6 CAF Super Cup titles, a record 4 African Cup Winners' Cup titles, an Afro-Asian Club Championship title, an Arab Club Champions Cup title, an Arab Cup Winners' Cup title, a record 2 Arab Super Cup titles, and won a bronze medal in the 2006 FIFA Club World Cup. Al Ahly is the second most successful club in the world in terms of number of international titles (20) behind Real Madrid (26).[4]

Al-Ahly is considered one of the most famous Egyptian, Arab and African clubs in the game of football, where Al-Ahly won 20 continental championships and placed him in second place among the clubs of the world after Real Madrid, and Al-Ahly club got the Egyptian Football League shield 40 times (record), the last of which is a general shield 2017, and won the Egyptian Cup championship 36 times (record), the last of which was in 2017. The "National Club" or "Red Genie" was also called by the fans of the African Champions League eight times (record number), most recently on the tenth of November 2013, through which it qualified To the Club World Cup for the fifth time in its history, and it also won the Super Sucking Cup Irrigation 9 times (record) and the African Super Cup 6 times (record) in addition to the African Confederation Cup (Confederation Cup) once

Al-Ahly was founded on April 24, 1907 by many dignitaries in Egypt such as Omar Lotfi Bey, Amin Sami Pasha, Mustafa Kamel and Talaat Harb, and was established as a club for Egyptian national youth and since its establishment the Egyptians met to spend their time and play sports, and after that a board of directors was established for the club headed by The English Michel Michel, and in 1909 the first football stadium was established in the history of the club and this stadium evolved into its current name (Mukhtar Touch Stadium), and the first football team was established in 1911, and the number of games inside the Al-Ahly Club in 1916 reached 4 games which are football Football, tennis, billiards and gymnastics.

Mohamed Abu Trika, who was born in Giza Governorate, Egypt, is one of the most famous Arab football players, as he started his career with Al Tarsana Club, and then moved to Al-Ahly Club. He won the title of Best Player in Egypt for 2007 for the fourth time in a row. After 2004, 2005, 2006 and 2007, he set a new record in Egypt. Muhammad Abu Trika announced his official retirement on December 18, 2013. He then decided to stay out of the limelight for several years and then returned to the sports field as a sports analyst on Bein Sports channels.
