ISMAILY club or ISMAILY Club for Sports
-->The story of the establishment of the Ismaili club dates back to more than 90 years, specifically in 1920 when the idea of ​​establishing an Egyptian club in Ismailia appeared on the surface, but this idea did not see the light and appears on the ground until in 1921 when this club was built through self-help and through collecting donations from the people . The headquarters of this club was the place of the Friday market today and its name at that time was Al-Nahda Club and it was popularized in 1924 and then this club - Ismaili or Al-Nahda - became a member of the Egyptian Football Association in 1926. At the beginning of his reign, the club was very modest, as it was built with a mud-brick fence with one room for dressing and a soccer field, and the stadium was sandy and next to it a wooden stall with an area of ​​no more than four meters. In 1931 the wooden kiosk was removed and the land was cultivated with vetiver and a modest building containing two rooms was created, but this building was not suitable in Ismailia, so the people joined forces to create a new headquarters The club is fit for Ismailia, and this was achieved in 1943, and the club acquired a piece of land with an area of ​​about 15 thousand meters. The club moved to its new headquarters, which is the youth care building currently, and Ismaili was the first Egyptian club in Ismailia where all the clubs at that time were affiliated with foreign communities in Ismailia And the channel area

The costs of establishing the Ismaili Club amounted to 6453 pounds, and the families and merchants donated for its establishment, and each of the contractor Mohamed Ali Ahmed donated the amount of 357 pounds, Dr. Suleiman Eid and Saleh Eid at the amount of 500 pounds, and the pilgrim Muhammad Muhammad Suleiman in the amount of 100 pounds, and the same amount Mr. Abu Zaid Al Minyawi, Sheikh Ahmad Atta for 75 pounds, Ahmed Zekry and Abdel Rahman Al-Sajai for 50 pounds, Hajj Muhammad Sahmoud and Fahmy Mikhail for 30 pounds, Hajj Ahmed Ali Abu Zaid al-Minyawi at 25 pounds, and Al-Khawajah in Nayati Vasolis for 20 pounds.

The first match was at the new stadium between the Ismaili club and the English army team (Canal Ariel). The new club was opened on Sunday, April 11, 1947, and a big celebration was held for this occasion and the opening ceremony was attended by Muhammad Haider Pasha Yawar, King Farouk as his representative, and Haider Pasha was at that time The head of the Egyptian Football Association and the representative of the National Sports Committee, and the assistant referee of the channel in the Ismailia city of the district, Mohamed Farid, cut the tape on behalf of the governor of the channel.

The opening match was held on the new club between the Ismaili (Ismailia) and Farouk Al-Awal club (Zamalek) and ended with the victory of Ismaili 3/2. Hubls, Mr. Charlie and Awad Abdel Rahman, and Captain Helmy Mustafa was the Ismaili coach at the time. Al-Ismaili Club was founded in 1921 under the name of Al-Nahda Sports Club.
