Zamalek Club or Zamalek Club for Sports
--> (established January 5, 1911 under the name Nile Palace Club) is a sports and social club, considered one of the oldest Egyptian and Arab clubs, with many sports.
The club's football team is one of the most prominent Egyptian and African clubs, which won the African Champions League (formerly the Champions League Clubs) five times, and managed to win the African Clubs Cup Championship once, and Zamalek won the African Super Cup three times, and also won the league The Egyptian year is 12 times, the Egypt Cup 27 times, and the Egyptian Super Cup three times. Zamalek football team won the title of best club in the world according to the International Federation of Football History and Statistics (English: IFFHS) in February 2003. It was also the first Egyptian team to qualify for the FIFA Club World Cup 2001 in Spain, a championship that did not exist due to problems related to funding for it .

The Egyptian club Zamalek appeared on the African football scene through the cup champions in 1976, and in the round of sixteen, it was the first African confrontation against Al-Ahly Club, Tripoli, in Al-Faiharah, and won back with three clean goals. Zamalek is famous for its steadfastness in its primary colors, which have not changed throughout the club’s history since 1911, as it is characterized by the white robe with two parallel lines in red. By displaying the team's shirt to the chest, the white color symbolizes the team's uniform for peace while the red lines indicate the struggle for victory.

He was ranked number one in the world in the monthly classification of the International Federation of Football History and Statistics for the month of February 2003 and was the first Egyptian club to receive this honor. He held the record for winning the Afro-Asian Club Championship after winning it twice.
